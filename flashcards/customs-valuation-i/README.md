## Key Topics Covered

### 1. Introduction to Customs Valuation
- What is customs value
- Why it's important
- Ad valorem duties
- Historical problems with arbitrary systems

### 2. Historical Development
- GATT Article VII foundation
- Brussels Definition of Value (BDV)
- Tokyo Round positive system
- Uruguay Round WTO Agreement

### 3. Objectives
- Fair, uniform, and neutral system
- Prevention of arbitrary values
- Preference for transaction value
- Simple and equitable criteria
- Commercial practice consistency

### 4. Six Valuation Methods (Sequential)
1. **Transaction value** - Primary method
2. **Identical goods** - Transaction value of identical goods
3. **Similar goods** - Transaction value of similar goods
4. **Deductive value** - Based on resale price
5. **Computed value** - Based on cost of production
6. **Fallback method** - Reasonable means

### 5. Transaction Value Elements
Three key elements:
- The sale
- Sale for export to country of importation
- Price actually paid or payable

### 6. Concept of Sale
What constitutes a sale:
- Transfer of ownership
- Specified price
- Buyer agreement

What doesn't qualify:
- Consignment sales
- Branch office transfers
- Hire/leasing contracts
- Goods on loan
- Goods for destruction
- Barter (case by case)

### 7. Price Actually Paid or Payable
- Total payment to seller
- Direct and indirect payments
- Discounts allowed (cash, volume, trade)
- Exclusions: dividends, buyer's marketing, warranties (if separate)

### 8. Legal Deductions (if distinguished)
- Post-importation construction/assembly
- Post-importation maintenance
- Post-importation technical assistance
- Duties and taxes of importing country
- Transport after importation

### 9. Article 1.1 Tests (Four Questions)
a) Restrictions on disposition or use?
b) Sale/price subject to undeterminable conditions?
c) Proceeds accrue to seller?
d) Buyer and seller related?

### 10. Allowable Restrictions
- Imposed by law/public authorities
- Limit geographical resale area
- Don't substantially affect value

### 11. Related Party Criteria (8 types)
1. Family members
2. Business partners
3. Employer-employee
4. Officers/directors
5. 5%+ voting stock by third person
6. One controls the other
7. Both controlled by third person
8. Together control third person

Key: Relationship alone doesn't disqualify transaction value

### 12. Related Party Examination
Two approaches:
- Circumstances of sale
- Test values

Proofs of no price influence:
- Consistent with industry practice
- Same as sales to unrelated buyers
- Sufficient to recover costs plus profit

### 13. Article 8 Adjustments (if not included)
Must add if:
- Criteria met
- Incurred by buyer
- Not already in price
- Based on objective and quantifiable data

### 14. Commissions and Brokerage
- Selling commissions: ADD
- Buying commissions: EXCLUDE
- Brokerage: Depends on facts

### 15. Containers and Packaging
- Containers treated as one with goods: ADD
- Packing materials and labor: ADD
- Retail packing examples: bags, boxes, blister packs

### 16. Assists (Four Categories)
1. **Materials/components** - Incorporated in goods
2. **Tools/dies/moulds** - Used in production
3. **Materials consumed** - In production
4. **Engineering/development** - Design, artwork, plans

Valuation:
- Cost of production (if by buyer/related person)
- Adjust for prior use
- Apportion reasonably

### 17. Royalties and Licence Fees
Three tests (all must be met):
1. Relate to imported goods
2. Paid as condition of sale
3. Not already in price

IP rights: patents, trademarks, copyrights

Not added:
- Right to reproduce in importing country
- Right to distribute (unless condition of sale)

### 18. Optional Adjustments (Article 8.2)
- Transport to port/place of importation
- Loading/unloading/handling charges
- Insurance costs

Incoterms importance:
- CIF: Usually included
- FOB: Usually need to add

### 19. Documentation Requirements
Key documents:
- Commercial invoice (most important)
- Bill of lading (shipment details)
- Packing list (quantity verification)
- Purchase order (terms confirmation)
- Proforma invoice (preliminary terms)
- Sales contract (contractual terms)

Document purposes:
- Support clearance
- Establish transaction value
- Verify price and terms
- Confirm origin
- Enable post-clearance audit

### 20. Objective and Quantifiable Data
- All adjustments must be verifiable
- No guesswork or subjective estimates
- If not quantifiable, adjustment cannot be made
- Documentation essential

## Transaction Value Formula

**Transaction Value = Price Actually Paid or Payable + Article 8 Adjustments**

Where:
- Price = Total payment to seller (direct + indirect)
- For the goods being valued
- When sold for export to country of importation
- Plus only permitted Article 8 additions
- Subject to Article 1 conditions
