## Key Topics Covered

### 1. Purpose and Objectives
- Fair, uniform, and neutral valuation system
- Prevention of arbitrary customs values
- Protection of negotiated tariff concessions
- Balance between trade facilitation and customs control

### 2. Six Valuation Methods (Sequential)
1. **Transaction Value** - Price actually paid or payable
2. **Identical Goods** - Transaction value of identical goods
3. **Similar Goods** - Transaction value of similar goods
4. **Deductive Value** - Based on resale price
5. **Computed Value** - Based on cost of production
6. **Fall-back Method** - Reasonable means consistent with principles

### 3. Transaction Value Components
- Price actually paid or payable
- For the goods
- When sold for export
- Plus Article 8 adjustments

### 4. Article 8 Adjustments
Additions to transaction value (if not already included):
- Commissions and brokerage (except buying commissions)
- Containers and packaging
- Assists (materials, tools, engineering)
- Royalties and licence fees
- Proceeds of subsequent resale
- International transport costs

### 5. Related Party Rules
Definition of related parties:
- Family members
- Business partners
- Employer-employee relationships
- Officers/directors
- 5%+ voting stock ownership
- Control relationships

Key principle: Relationship alone doesn't disqualify transaction value

### 6. Prohibited Methods
Cannot base customs value on:
- Selling price of domestic goods
- Higher of two alternative values
- Domestic market price in exporting country
- Cost of production (except computed value)
- Export price to third country
- Minimum customs values
- Arbitrary or fictitious values

### 7. Procedural Rights
- Right to explanation of valuation determination
- Right to appeal (Article 11)
- Confidentiality protection (Article 10)
- Release of goods provisions (Article 13)
- Publication requirements (Article 12)

### 8. Administrative Framework
- WTO Valuation Committee (Geneva) - Policy issues
- Technical Committee on Customs Valuation (WCO Brussels) - Technical matters
- Notification requirements for transparency
- Review and dispute settlement mechanisms
