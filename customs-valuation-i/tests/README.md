## Key Concepts to Master

### Transaction Value Formula
**Transaction Value = Price Actually Paid or Payable + Article 8 Adjustments**

### Three Key Elements
1. The sale
2. Sale for export to country of importation
3. Price actually paid or payable

### Article 1.1 Tests (Four Questions)
a) Restrictions on disposition or use?
b) Sale/price subject to undeterminable conditions?
c) Proceeds accrue to seller?
d) Buyer and seller related?

### Related Party Criteria (8 types)
1. Family members
2. Business partners
3. Employer-employee
4. Officers/directors
5. 5%+ voting stock by third person
6. One controls the other
7. Both controlled by third person
8. Together control third person

### Article 8 Adjustments (if not included)
1. Commissions and brokerage (except buying commissions)
2. Containers and packaging
3. Assists (materials, tools, consumed materials, engineering)
4. Royalties and licence fees
5. Proceeds of subsequent resale
6. Optional: transport, loading/unloading, insurance

### Four Categories of Assists
1. Materials, components, parts incorporated in goods
2. Tools, dies, moulds used in production
3. Materials consumed in production
4. Engineering, development, artwork, design work

### Six Valuation Methods (Sequential)
1. Transaction value
2. Transaction value of identical goods
3. Transaction value of similar goods
4. Deductive value
5. Computed value
6. Fallback method
