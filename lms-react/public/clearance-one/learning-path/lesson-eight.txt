Topic 7: Entry of Goods and Supporting Documents

Topic goal: By the end of this topic, the learner should be able to:
- explain when goods are deemed entered,
- state the timelines for entry,
- identify the main purposes for which goods may be entered,
- recognize the key supporting documents,
- explain Form C36 and provisional entry,
- identify goods that may be cleared without entry.

Topic total XP: 600 base XP 
Mastery bonus: +50 XP 
Perfect boss bonus: +100 XP

Step 1: Learn
Purpose: introduce entry of goods in simple, structured form.
Lesson format: 6 learning cards + 4 micro-checks
Estimated time: 4–6 minutes 
Pass type: completion-based 
XP: up to 100

Learning cards
Card 1: When goods are deemed entered
- entry is made and lodged in the prescribed manner
- duty due or deposit is paid, or security is given
- this comes from Section 2(2)(a)

Card 2: Entry for home consumption
- goods are deemed entered for home consumption when declared for use in a Partner State other than temporary use
- Section 2(2)(b)

Card 3: Time limit for entry
- goods must be entered within 21 days after commencement of discharge
- for vehicles, on arrival
- further time may be allowed by the proper officer

Card 4: Pre-arrival processing and purposes of entry
- goods may be entered before arrival
- entry purposes include:
  * home consumption
  * warehousing
  * transhipment
  * transit
  * export processing zones

Card 5: Supporting documents
- bill of lading / airway bill / road manifest
- packing list
- commercial invoice
- freight invoice
- insurance debit note
- certificate of origin
- permits / licenses
- proof of payment
- and more

Card 6: Special documents and exceptions
- Form C36 = declaration of value
- Certificate A = importer not related to supplier
- Certificate B = importer related to supplier
- provisional entry allowed if documents/information are missing
- some goods may be cleared without entry

Bonus: Goods that may be cleared without entry
- mail bags and postal articles
- bona fide personal baggage of passengers
- human remains
- diplomatic bags

May be cleared without entry subject to undertaking to furnish entry within 48 hours:
- bullion
- currency notes
- coin
- perishable goods

Micro-check examples
- When are goods deemed entered?
- What is the 21-day rule?
- What is Form C36?
- Name one purpose for which goods may be entered.

Pass rule
Complete all cards and answer at least 3 out of 4 micro-checks correctly.
