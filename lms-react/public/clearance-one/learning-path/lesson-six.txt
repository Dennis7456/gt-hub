Topic 5: Clearance of Vessels and Aircraft

Topic goal: By the end of this topic, the learner should be able to:
- explain the basic clearance process for vessels and aircraft,
- identify the main arrival documents and forms,
- distinguish the purpose of each list and declaration,
- explain the rules on dutiable stores and seals,
- state when a certificate of clearance is issued.

Topic total XP: 600 base XP 
Mastery bonus: +50 XP 
Perfect boss bonus: +100 XP

Step 1: Learn
Purpose: introduce the clearance process and key forms.
Lesson format: 6 learning cards + 4 micro-checks
Estimated time: 4–6 minutes 
Pass type: completion-based 
XP: up to 100

Learning cards
Card 1: Basic clearance duty
- the master or agent of every aircraft or vessel must produce books and documents relating to the craft, cargo, stores, baggage, crew, and passengers

Card 2: Inward reports
- Form C2 for vessels
- Form C4 for aircraft
- contains details like nationality, tonnage, crew, and port of landing

Card 3: Main lists
- Form C3 parcels list
- crew list
- Form C5 passenger list

Card 4: Stores and crew declarations
- Form C6 declaration/advice of consumable stores
- Form C7 crew declaration of dutiable goods

Card 5: Other lists
- last port of call list
- arms and ammunition list
- narcotics list
- cargo manifest expected before arrival under Section 24

Card 6: Seals and clearance
- dutiable stores may be sealed
- allowable duty-free stores per crew member
- breaking seal without approval is an offence
- certificate of clearance issued when legal requirements are met

Micro-check examples
- Which form is inward report for a vessel?
- Which form is inward report for an aircraft?
- Which form covers crew declaration?
- What may happen to excess dutiable stores?

Pass rule
Complete all cards and get at least 3 out of 4 micro-checks correct.

Reward
70 XP for completion
+30 XP for full micro-check accuracy

Step 3: Match and Distinguish
Purpose: separate the forms and lists that look similar.
Lesson format: 8 matching tasks + 4 distinction tasks
Estimated time: 5–7 minutes 
Pass threshold: 70% 
XP: up to 100

Matching tasks
Match the form/list to its use:
- C2 → inward report for vessel
- C4 → inward report for aircraft
- C3 → parcels list
- C5 → passenger list
- C6 → declaration/advice of consumable stores
- C7 → crew declaration
- last port of call list → previous ten port calls
- narcotics list → narcotics on board

Distinction tasks
Separate:
- C3 vs C5
- C6 vs C7
- crew list vs passenger list
- inward report vs cargo manifest

Example distinction question
Which statement is correct? 
A. C6 and C7 mean exactly the same thing 
B. C6 deals with dutiable stores on board, while C7 is the crew member's declaration of dutiable goods in his or her possession

Example scenarios

Scenario 2: An aircraft arrives and the officer asks for the list showing passengers disembarking and those remaining on board. Which form applies? A. C3 B. C6 C. C5 D. C2

Scenario 3: A crew member is carrying dutiable goods. Which form is most relevant to that declaration? A. C7 B. C6 C. C3 D. C17

Scenario 4: The proper officer is checking all dutiable consumable stores on board. Which form is most relevant? A. C5 B. C6 C. C2 D. C36

Scenario 5: There are dutiable stores beyond the allowable quantity for crew use. What may the proper officer do? A. Ignore them automatically B. Place part or all of the stores under seal C. Convert them into passenger baggage D. Send them to a railway station

Scenario 6: A crew member breaks a Customs seal on stores without approval. Under the notes, this is: A. acceptable if the stores are small B. an offence C. allowed if reported later D. treated as a provisional entry

Key facts this topic should reinforce
These are the facts the learner should know cold by the end:
- C2 = inward report for vessel
- C4 = inward report for aircraft
- C3 = parcels list
- C5 = passenger list
- C6 = declaration/advice of consumable stores
- C7 = crew declaration
- cargo manifest should be submitted before arrival under Section 24
- excess dutiable stores may be sealed
- breaking the seal without approval is an offence
- a certificate of clearance is issued once the proper officer is satisfied requirements are met

Why Topic 5 matters
This topic is where the learner starts to think like a real clearance officer or declarant. It teaches:
- which document is needed,
- why each list exists,
- how stores are controlled,
- and when legal clearance may be granted.

It is one of the most important "forms mastery" zones in the chapter.
