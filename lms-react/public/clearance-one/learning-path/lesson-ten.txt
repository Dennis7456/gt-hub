Topic 9: Partner Government Agencies (PGAs)

Topic goal: By the end of this topic, the learner should be able to:
- explain what PGAs are,
- identify the main PGAs mentioned in the notes,
- distinguish regulatory, security, and tax-related roles,
- match goods or situations to the relevant agency,
- explain why Customs does not work alone at the border.

Topic total XP: 600 base XP
Mastery bonus: +50 XP
Perfect boss bonus: +100 XP

Step 1: Learn
Purpose: introduce PGAs and their border roles simply.
Lesson format: 6 learning cards + 4 micro-checks
Estimated time: 4–6 minutes
Pass type: completion-based
XP: up to 100

Learning cards

Card 1: What are PGAs?
- PGAs means Partner Government Agencies
- they were previously referred to as OGAs
- Customs works with them in clearance of goods and services through borders

Card 2: Why PGAs matter
- some goods need more than Customs control
- different agencies regulate standards, health, safety, wildlife, biosafety, security, and other specialized issues

Card 3: Examples of PGAs from the notes
- KEBS
- KEPHIS
- KPA
- NIS
- KPS
- Immigration Department

Card 4: More PGAs from the notes
- Port Health
- Kenya Radiation Authority
- KMA
- NBA
- KWS
- ACA

Card 5: Broad functions of PGAs
- regulatory functions
- security functions
- tax collection functions

Card 6: Practical clearance meaning
- some agencies issue permits or approvals
- some inspect goods
- some support security screening
- some enforce specialized national laws

Micro-check examples
- What does PGA stand for?
- Name one PGA in the notes.
- Name one broad function of PGAs.
- Why does Customs work with PGAs?

Pass rule
Complete all cards and answer at least 3 out of 4 micro-checks correctly.

Step 3: Match and Distinguish
Purpose: help the learner match agencies to likely functions and distinguish role types.
Lesson format: 8 matching tasks + 4 distinction tasks
Estimated time: 5–7 minutes
Pass threshold: 70%
XP: up to 100

Matching tasks
Match the PGA to its likely area of concern based on the notes:
- KEBS → standards/regulatory control
- KEPHIS → plant health/phytosanitary control
- KPA → port operations interface
- NIS → security/intelligence
- KPS → policing/security
- Immigration Department → movement/control of persons
- Port Health → public health control
- KWS → wildlife-related control

Distinction tasks
Separate:
- regulatory role vs security role
- Customs role vs PGA specialized role
- standards agency vs wildlife agency
- biosafety/radiation/health-related oversight

Example distinction question
Which statement is correct?
A. All PGAs perform exactly the same role
B. PGAs may perform regulatory, security, or tax collection functions depending on their mandate
C. Customs replaces PGAs entirely
D. PGAs are only relevant after goods leave the border

Why Topic 9 matters
This topic teaches the learner that Customs clearance is not a one-office activity. Many goods require:
- technical approval,
- standards checks,
- health checks,
- wildlife control,
- biosafety control,
- or security screening.

So Topic 9 builds the idea of border coordination, which is essential both in exams and in real practice.
