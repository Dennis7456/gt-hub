Topic 6: Amendments, Short Landed, Excess Landed, and Coasting

Topic goal: By the end of this topic, the learner should be able to:
- explain when an inward report may be amended,
- identify the documents and fee for amendment,
- distinguish short landed from excess landed cargo,
- explain the supplementary manifest process,
- define coasting vessels/aircraft and transire,
- apply these rules in practical Customs scenarios.

Step 1: Learn
Purpose: introduce the irregular cargo situations and coasting rules simply.
Lesson format: 6 learning cards + 4 micro-checks
Estimated time: 4–6 minutes 
Pass type: completion-based 
XP: up to 100

Learning cards
Card 1: Amendment of inward report
- the proper officer may permit amendment of destination, ownership, or status of goods
- obvious errors or omissions may also be amended

Card 2: Amendment process
- shipping line/airline agent submits request
- details are filled in
- supporting documents attached
- reasons given online
- officer approves
- amendment fee paid

Card 3: Supporting documents and fee
- manifest corrector
- affidavit
- fee: USD 10

Card 4: Short landed cargo
- manifested cargo that is not physically on board for unloading

Card 5: Excess landed cargo
- cargo not manifested but physically on board for unloading
- handled through a supplementary manifest
- supplementary manifest fee: USD 10

Card 6: Coasting vessels and aircraft
- goods moved by sea or air from one part of a Partner State to another are carried coastwise
- the authorizing document is transire
- transire also serves as clearance for coastwise voyage

Micro-check examples
- What is short landed cargo?
- What is excess landed cargo?
- What document regularizes excess landed cargo?
- What authorizes coastwise carriage?

Pass rule
Complete all cards and answer at least 3 out of 4 micro-checks correctly.

Key facts this topic should reinforce
By the end, the learner should know:
- inward reports may be amended for destination, ownership, status of goods, and obvious errors/omissions
- amendment support documents: manifest corrector + affidavit
- amendment fee: USD 10
- short landed cargo = manifested but physically absent for unloading
- excess landed cargo = not manifested but physically present for unloading
- excess landed cargo is handled by supplementary manifest
- supplementary manifest fee: USD 10
- coastwise carriage means movement by sea or air from one part of a Partner State to another
- transire authorizes coastwise carriage and serves as clearance for that voyage
