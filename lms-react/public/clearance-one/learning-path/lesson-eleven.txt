Topic 10: Sources of Customs Law and the East African Community

Topic goal: By the end of this topic, the learner should be able to:
- state the sources of Customs law,
- explain the EAC historical and legal background,
- identify key Treaty and Protocol dates,
- name the organs of the EAC and their roles,
- distinguish EACCMA, EACCMR 2010, and the Compliance and Enforcement Regulations 2012.

Mastery bonus: +50 XP

Step 1: Learn
Purpose: introduce the legal and regional framework clearly.
Lesson format: 6 learning cards + 4 micro-checks
Estimated time: 4–6 minutes
Pass type: completion-based
XP: up to 100

Learning cards

Card 1: Sources of Customs law under Article 39
- relevant provisions of the Treaty establishing the EAC
- Protocol on the Establishment of the EAC Customs Union and its annexes
- regulations and directives made by the Council
- applicable court decisions
- Acts of the Community enacted by the Legislative Assembly
- relevant principles of international law

Card 2: What is a common market?
- a group of countries working together mainly to promote trade and common interests
- another name used in the notes: regional economic grouping

Card 3: EAC history basics
- 1967: EAC formed by Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania
- reasons for formation: trade, unity, common market, free movement, fair/reduced taxes
- 1977: collapse due to political and economic differences

Card 4: Revival and legal dates
- 4 March 1996: revival/rebirth of the EAC
- Treaty signed: 30 November 1999
- Treaty came into force: 7 July 2000
- Customs Union Protocol signed: 2 March 2004 in Arusha

Card 5: Membership expansion
- Rwanda and Burundi joined in 2008 and started applying instruments in July 2009
- South Sudan joined in 2016

Card 6: Core Customs legal instruments
- EACCMA, 2004
- EACCMR, 2010
- EACCMA Compliance and Enforcement Regulations, 2012

Micro-check examples
- Name one source of Customs law.
- Which three countries formed the EAC in 1967?
- When was the Customs Union Protocol signed?
- Name one major Customs legal instrument.

Pass rule
Complete all cards and answer at least 3 out of 4 correctly.

Bonus mini-section inside Topic 10: EAC organs
This should appear in review or boss mode.

The learner should know the organs listed in the notes:
- Summit → Heads of State; gives general direction
- Council → policy organ
- Coordination Committee → coordinates and submits reports/recommendations
- Sectoral Committees → prepare and monitor implementation
- East African Court of Justice → judicial body ensuring adherence to law
- East African Legislative Assembly → legislative organ
- Secretariat → executive organ

This is a strong exam area.
