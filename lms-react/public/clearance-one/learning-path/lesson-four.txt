Topic 3: Arrival and Reporting of Cargo

Topic goal: By the end of this topic, the learner should be able to:
- identify the modes by which cargo arrives,
- explain reporting obligations on arrival,
- apply Section 29, Section 30, Section 31, and Section 32 correctly,
- distinguish responsibilities for road, rail, other means, and pipeline cargo,
- answer practical arrival questions quickly and accurately.

Topic total XP: 600 base XP 
Mastery bonus: +50 XP 
Perfect boss bonus: +100 XP

Lesson format: 6 learning cards + 4 micro-checks
Estimated time: 4–6 minutes 
Pass type: completion-based 
XP: up to 100

Learning cards
Card 1: Main modes of arrival
- overland
- by air
- by sea

Card 2: Overland means of conveyance
- motor vehicles
- trains
- pipeline
- animal
- cables

Card 3: Section 29
- person in charge of a vehicle arriving overland
- must report arrival
- furnish information
- declare truth of particulars
- answer questions
- produce documents
- make due entry

Card 4: Section 30
- railway station procedures
- station master's role
- invoices, consignment notes, waybills

Card 5: Section 31
- other means of conveyance apart from vehicle and train
- similar obligations to Section 29

Card 6: Section 32
- wet cargo by pipeline
- petroleum and gas
- record nature and quantity
- provide apparatus for proper recordkeeping

Micro-check examples

- Which section applies to wet cargo by pipeline?
- Who acts on behalf of the railway company?

Step 3: Match and Distinguish
Purpose: help the learner separate similar sections and duties.
Lesson format: 8 matching tasks + 4 distinction tasks
Estimated time: 5–7 minutes 
Pass threshold: 70% 
XP: up to 100

Matching tasks
Match the section to its function:
- Section 29 → vehicle arriving overland
- Section 30 → railway station duties
- Section 31 → other means apart from vehicle and train
- Section 32 → wet cargo by pipeline

Match the duty to the correct person:
- report arrival at frontier port → person in charge of vehicle
- submit invoices/waybills → station master
- record petroleum quantities → pipeline operator
- comply like Section 29 even without vehicle/train → person using other means

Distinction tasks
Separate:
- Section 29 vs Section 31
- Section 30 vs Section 32
- road cargo vs rail cargo
- general other means vs wet cargo by pipeline

Example distinction question
Which statement is correct? 
A. Section 31 applies only to wet cargo 
B. Section 29 applies to overland vehicles, while Section 31 extends similar obligations to other means apart from vehicle and train 
C. Section 30 applies to pipelines 
D. Section 32 applies to railway wagons

70 XP for pass
+10 XP for first-half perfect
+20 XP for full accuracy

Step 4: Scenario Challenge
Purpose: apply the rules to simple border situations.
Lesson format: 6 scenario questions
Estimated time: 6–8 minutes 
Pass threshold: 70% 
XP: up to 100

Example scenarios
Scenario 1: A truck arrives at a frontier port carrying imported goods. What is the first obligation under the notes? A. Deliver goods to consignee B. Report arrival to the officer stationed at the frontier port C. Submit passenger list D. Pay warehouse rent

Scenario 2: A person crosses with goods using donkeys instead of a vehicle. Which rule applies? A. No Customs duty exists B. Section 31 applies with obligations similar to Section 29 C. Section 30 applies D. Goods are treated as baggage automatically

Scenario 3: An international train enters the Partner State with cargo. Who performs Customs formalities on behalf of the railway company? A. The importer B. The station master or person in charge C. The clearing agent only D. The consignee

Scenario 4: A pipeline operator imports petroleum but fails to keep proper records of quantity. Which section is most directly relevant? A. Section 29 B. Section 30 C. Section 31 D. Section 32

Scenario 5: Goods are removed from the railway Customs area without permission of the proper officer. This is: A. normal release B. an offence C. a provisional entry D. a home-use declaration

Scenario 6: A person brings goods through an unappointed route without permission. What requirement has been breached? A. Goods should be brought through a port appointed under Section 11 unless otherwise permitted B. Goods should first go to an EPZ C. Goods should only travel by rail D. Goods should be declared after delivery

Why Topic 3 matters
This topic is the first real procedure zone. It teaches the learner that Customs is not just definitions — it is also about:
- who reports,
- where they report,
- what documents they submit,
- and what happens when procedure is ignored.

That makes it the bridge between vocabulary and operational Customs practice.
