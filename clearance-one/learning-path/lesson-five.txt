Topic 4: Rummaging and Search

Topic goal: By the end of this topic, the learner should be able to:
- explain what rummaging is,
- identify the legal basis for rummaging and search,
- describe the correct order of search,
- explain the rules for searching persons,
- state what happens when undeclared goods are found,
- distinguish rummage of vessels, coastal vessels, and aircraft.

Step 1: Learn
Purpose: introduce rummaging clearly and simply.
Lesson format: 6 learning cards + 4 micro-checks
Estimated time: 4–6 minutes 
Pass type: completion-based 
XP: up to 100

Learning cards
Card 1: What is rummaging?
- an intense search of a vessel or aircraft
- usually done on a risk basis

Card 2: Legal authority
- Section 246 of EACCMA gives the proper officer power to search aircraft or vessels
- Regulation 25 requires crew assistance

Card 3: Starting a rummage
- inform the Master, or in his absence the Chief Officer
- request a ship's officer to accompany the squad
- crew may be required to assist with dismantling if needed

Card 4: Order of search
- crew quarters first
- then passenger/public areas
- then storerooms, galleys, lockers, pantries
- then holds, tunnels, engine rooms, bunker spaces, concealment points
- structural fittings only with care

Card 5: Search of persons
- officer must have reasonable grounds
- if objection is raised, the person may be taken before a senior officer
- females can only be searched by females
- search must happen in private

Card 6: What happens if undeclared goods are found
- note concealment carefully
- seize the goods
- report to officer in charge
- show Master
- trace owner if possible
- mark goods and take them to Customs warehouse

Micro-check examples
- Which section gives power to search vessels or aircraft?
- Who must be informed before vessel rummage?
- Which area is searched first?
- Who may search a female suspect?

Pass rule
Complete all cards and answer at least 3 out of 4 micro-checks correctly.

Step 3: Match and Distinguish
Purpose: separate similar rules and roles.
Lesson format: 8 matching tasks + 4 distinction tasks
Estimated time: 5–7 minutes 
Pass threshold: 70% 
XP: up to 100

Matching tasks
Match the item to the correct rule:
- Section 246 → power to search aircraft or vessels
- Regulation 25 → crew must assist rummaging officers
- Section 155 → search of persons when reasonable grounds exist
- Customs warehouse → where seized goods are taken

Match the stage to the action:
- before rummage begins → inform Master/Chief Officer
- during crew search → compare with Form C.9
- if undeclared goods found → seize and report
- after completion → report to officer in charge/station

Distinction tasks
Separate:
- rummage of vessel vs search of person
- vessel rummage vs aircraft rummage
- scheduled foreign flights vs non-scheduled flights
- undeclared goods vs goods declared later

Example distinction question
Which statement is correct? 
A. All aircraft from foreign must always be rummaged fully 
B. Scheduled services should be kept to a minimum consistent with revenue control, but non-scheduled, charter, and private aircraft from foreign should be rummaged mandatorily 
C. Vessel rummage never requires crew assistance 
D. Search of persons may be done in public

Why Topic 4 matters
This topic shifts the learner from normal arrival procedure into enforcement logic. It teaches:
- when officers may search,
- how they must search,
- what safeguards protect people,
- and how undeclared goods are handled after discovery.

That makes it one of the most practical operational topics in the chapter.
