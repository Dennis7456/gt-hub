Topic 2: Import Procedures and Customs Terms

Topic goal: By the end of this topic, the learner should be able to:
- explain the main import categories,
- define core Customs terms,
- distinguish similar terms correctly,
- identify where each term applies in practice,
- use the terms confidently in questions and scenarios.

Topic total XP: 600 base XP 
Mastery bonus: +50 XP 
Perfect boss bonus: +100 XP

Step 1: Learn
Purpose: introduce the topic vocabulary in simple form.
Lesson format: 6 short concept cards + 4 micro-checks
Estimated time: 4–6 minutes 
Pass type: completion-based 
XP: up to 100

Learning cards
Card 1: Import categories
- direct imports
- temporary imports
- re-imports
- warehoused goods

Card 2: Approved place of loading/unloading

Card 3: Boarding station

Card 4: Cargo and what is excluded from cargo

Card 5: Customs area, Customs warehouse, transit shed

Card 6: Port, proper officer, master, sufferance wharf, uncustomed goods

Micro-check examples
- Which one is an import category?
- Which term refers to a place appointed for Customs operations?
- What is excluded from cargo?
- Who is a proper officer?

Pass rule
Complete all cards and answer at least 3 out of 4 correctly.

Reward
70 XP for completion
+30 XP for getting all micro-checks correct

Step 3: Match and Distinguish
Purpose: train the learner to tell similar terms apart.
Lesson format: 8 matching tasks + 4 distinction tasks
Estimated time: 5–7 minutes 
Pass threshold: 70% 
XP: up to 100

Matching exercises
Match the term to the meaning:
- approved place of unloading → place appointed by Commissioner for loading/unloading
- boarding station → place for boarding/disembarkation of officers
- Customs warehouse → deposit of unentered, detained, seized, abandoned goods
- transit shed → premises appointed for goods under Customs control
- master → person in charge of aircraft or vessel
- proper officer → officer authorized to require/perform acts under the Act

Distinction tasks
Ask the learner to separate:
- Customs area vs Customs warehouse
- approved place vs sufferance wharf
- cargo vs bona fide personal baggage
- transit shed vs Customs warehouse

Example distinction question
Which statement is correct? 
A. A Customs warehouse and Customs area are the same 
B. A Customs area is for Customs operations generally, while a Customs warehouse is for deposit of certain goods 
C. A transit shed is only for passengers 
D. A sufferance wharf is always a port

Reward
70 XP for pass
+10 XP for first-half perfect
+20 XP for full accuracy

Step 4: Scenario Challenge
Purpose: move from definitions to use in practice.
Lesson format: 6 short scenarios
Estimated time: 6–8 minutes 
Pass threshold: 70% 
XP: up to 100

Example scenarios
Scenario 1: A shipment is being held because the goods are unentered and duties are not settled. Which place is most suitable under the notes? A. Boarding station B. Customs warehouse C. Passenger hall D. Port health office

Scenario 2: A place has been formally appointed for loading and unloading by the Commissioner. What is it called? A. Transit shed B. Approved place of loading/unloading C. Sufferance wharf only D. Customs area only

Scenario 3: An importer says the crew's suitcases are cargo. What is the best answer? A. Always true B. Bona fide personal baggage is excluded from cargo C. Cargo means baggage only D. Cargo means only sea freight

Scenario 4: Goods are loaded at a place that is not an approved place of loading, but the Commissioner specially allows it. What is that place called? A. Boarding station B. Sufferance wharf C. Customs airport D. Coasting port

Scenario 5: An officer lawfully requires performance of an act under the Act. Which term best describes that officer? A. Station master B. Master C. Proper officer D. Declarant

Scenario 6: A plane arrives carrying imported goods. For Customs law purposes, the "port" in relation to the aircraft is: A. any runway B. Customs airport C. transit shed D. bonded warehouse

Reward
70 XP for pass
+15 XP first try
+15 XP for all correct

Step 6: Boss Challenge
Purpose: prove mastery and unlock Topic 3.
Lesson format: 12 mixed questions
Estimated time: 7–10 minutes 
Pass threshold: 75% 
XP: up to 100

Question mix

- 3 distinction questions
- 3 practical scenarios
- 2 trap questions

Example boss questions
- Which term refers to goods whose full duties due have not been paid?
- Which one is excluded from cargo?
- Which term refers to a place appointed for deposit of unentered or seized goods?

- Which statement best describes a proper officer?
- A place is not an approved place of unloading, but the Commissioner allows unloading there. What is it?
- Trap: "All goods carried by a vessel are cargo."
