Topic 8: Transit Shed, Regimes, and Warehousing

Topic goal: By the end of this topic, the learner should be able to:
- explain the legal status of goods in a transit shed,
- identify who remains responsible for such goods,
- state the consequences of failing to account for goods,
- distinguish the main import Customs regimes,
- explain warehousing concepts, including warehousing in situ,
- compute and interpret Customs warehouse rent basics.

Step 1: Learn
Purpose: introduce transit shed responsibility, regimes, and warehousing basics clearly.
Lesson format: 6 learning cards + 4 micro-checks
Estimated time: 4–6 minutes
Pass type: completion-based
XP: up to 100

Learning cards

Card 1: Goods in a transit shed
- goods in a transit shed are deemed to be in the importing vessel or aircraft until delivered
- the owner or agent remains responsible

Card 2: Failure to account for goods
- if goods reported for discharge are not duly unloaded and deposited in a transit shed or Customs area, the master/agent must pay duty unless a satisfactory explanation is given
- failure to account for goods in custody is an offence

Card 3: Condemned goods and costs
- owner of vessel/aircraft, agent, or transit shed owner is liable for costs of reshipment or destruction of condemned goods
- failure to meet those costs is an offence

Card 4: Main import Customs regimes
- imports for home use
- temporary imports
- re-imports
- warehousing

Card 5: Sub-regimes and special movements
- ex-warehouse home use
- ex-EPZ home use
- ex-MUB home use
- entry into MUB/EPZ
- transfers between MUBs or EPZs

Card 6: Customs warehouse rent
- after the 21 working day period, goods may be deemed to be in a Customs warehouse
- Customs Warehouse Date is the 22nd working day from discharge
- rent formula uses CBM, rate, days, and exchange rate

Micro-check examples
- Who remains responsible for goods in a transit shed?
- What happens if goods are not duly unloaded?
- Name one import Customs regime.
- On which day does Customs warehouse status start?

Pass rule
Complete all cards and answer at least 3 out of 4 micro-checks correctly.

Step 3: Match and Distinguish
Purpose: separate related regimes and warehousing concepts.
Lesson format: 8 matching tasks + 4 distinction tasks
Estimated time: 5–7 minutes
Pass threshold: 70%
XP: up to 100

Matching tasks
Match the item to the correct meaning:
- transit shed → goods deemed still in importing vessel/aircraft until delivered
- home use → goods declared for use in a Partner State
- temporary importation → goods imported for return in unaltered state or repair
- re-importation → goods returning after temporary export or rejection/repair
- warehousing → deposit/storage under Customs warehousing regime
- MUB → manufacturing under bond movements
- EPZ → export processing zone movements
- Customs Warehouse Date → 22nd working day from discharge

Distinction tasks

- transit shed vs Customs warehouse
- home use vs warehousing
- temporary import vs re-import
- entered but not removed vs unentered cargo

Bonus mini-section inside Topic 8: Warehouse rent calculation basics
This should appear in the review or boss section.

The learner should know:
- 21 days are working days, not calendar days
- weekends and public holidays are excluded
- Customs Warehouse Date = 22nd working day from discharge
- rent formula: CBM × number of rent days × rate per CBM × exchange rate
- simplified expression from the notes: $0.3 × exchange rate × CBM × number of days

This is a great area for future calculation drills.

Key facts this topic should reinforce
By the end, the learner should know:
- goods in a transit shed are deemed to remain in the importing vessel/aircraft until delivery
- owner or agent remains responsible
- failure to account for goods in custody is an offence
- master/agent may have to pay duty if goods are not duly unloaded and deposited, unless explanation is satisfactory
- costs of reshipment or destruction of condemned goods may fall on the owner/agent/transit shed owner
- main regimes are:
  * home use
  * temporary imports
  * re-imports
  * warehousing
- special regimes include ex-warehouse home use, ex-EPZ, ex-MUB, and transfers between EPZs/MUBs
- Customs warehouse date is the 22nd working day
- warehouse rent is based on space, time, rate, and exchange rate

Why Topic 8 matters
This topic teaches the learner what happens to goods after arrival but before final release, especially where custody, delay, storage, and liability are involved. It is important because many exam and practice questions turn on:
- who is still responsible,
- when goods are deemed warehoused,
- and when storage charges start.

It is one of the most practical operations topics in the chapter.
